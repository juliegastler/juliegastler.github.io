Mrs. Gastler's MCP Data

 

Important notes:

  • I did not implement MCP until Spring of last school year. While I did have some elements during the fall, it was limited to organization and checkpoints. I had started sharing instructional videos, but not requiring students to watch them, they were just there.
  • Last year's students were allowed to retake tests but this year's students were not.
  • Last year, the Unit 1 test was graded with the parts of the test being weighted proportional to the points in each part. Units 2 and 3 were weighted 50/50. This year, all tests are weighted proportionally.
  • All tests this year have been modified to remove excess low level questions and up the rigor, including increasing the number of short answer questions in which students are asked to explain their reasoning. Students last year did not have to do this.

As a result of these things, I'm not sure comparing the values is all that meaningfull. But, the trends in time, the progression from Unit 1 to Unit 3 when comparing last year to this year fascinate me. It actually matches my observations from previous years:

  • Unit 1 is very easy, and
  • It takes time for students to "get" MCP, but once they do, it's wonderful.

    • I can't wait to see what next semester's data looks like.
